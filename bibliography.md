# Bibliography and References

A comprehensive collection of foundational works, research papers, and books that inform and support the Syndicate framework.

---

## Foundational Mathematics and Statistics

### Learning Theory

1. **Vapnik, V. N.** (1998). *Statistical Learning Theory*. Wiley-Interscience.
   - Foundational work on VC dimension, structural risk minimization, and support vector machines.

2. **Shalev-Shwartz, S., & Ben-David, S.** (2014). *Understanding Machine Learning: From Theory to Algorithms*. Cambridge University Press.
   - Comprehensive introduction to PAC learning, sample complexity, and generalization bounds.

3. **Mohri, M., Rostamizadeh, A., & Talwalkar, A.** (2018). *Foundations of Machine Learning* (2nd ed.). MIT Press.
   - Covers Rademacher complexity, algorithmic stability, and kernel methods.

4. **Hastie, T., Tibshirani, R., & Friedman, J.** (2009). *The Elements of Statistical Learning: Data Mining, Inference, and Prediction* (2nd ed.). Springer.
   - Definitive reference on statistical learning, regularization, and model selection.

### Information Theory

5. **Cover, T. M., & Thomas, J. A.** (2006). *Elements of Information Theory* (2nd ed.). Wiley-Interscience.
   - Shannon entropy, mutual information, data processing inequality, and coding theory.

6. **MacKay, D. J.** (2003). *Information Theory, Inference, and Learning Algorithms*. Cambridge University Press.
   - Connects information theory, Bayesian inference, and machine learning.

---

## Optimization and Decision Theory

7. **Bertsekas, D. P.** (2017). *Dynamic Programming and Optimal Control* (4th ed.). Athena Scientific.
   - Markov decision processes, value iteration, policy optimization.

8. **Boyd, S., & Vandenberghe, L.** (2004). *Convex Optimization*. Cambridge University Press.
   - Linear programming, quadratic programming, semidefinite programming.

9. **Sutton, R. S., & Barto, A. G.** (2018). *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction* (2nd ed.). MIT Press.
   - Temporal difference learning, Q-learning, policy gradient methods.

---

## Computational Complexity and Limits

10. **Arora, S., & Barak, B.** (2009). *Computational Complexity: A Modern Approach*. Cambridge University Press.
    - P vs NP, NP-completeness, approximation algorithms.

11. **Sipser, M.** (2012). *Introduction to the Theory of Computation* (3rd ed.). Cengage Learning.
    - Turing machines, decidability, Halting problem.

12. **Gödel, K.** (1931). "Über formal unentscheidbare Sätze der Principia Mathematica und verwandter Systeme I". *Monatshefte für Mathematik und Physik*, 38(1), 173-198.
    - Original incompleteness theorems paper.

13. **Turing, A. M.** (1936). "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem". *Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society*, 42(1), 230-265.
    - Foundational paper on computability and the Halting problem.

---

## No-Free-Lunch and Impossibility Results

14. **Wolpert, D. H., & Macready, W. G.** (1997). "No Free Lunch Theorems for Optimization". *IEEE Transactions on Evolutionary Computation*, 1(1), 67-82.
    - Proves no algorithm dominates all others across all problems.

15. **Wolpert, D. H.** (1996). "The Lack of A Priori Distinctions Between Learning Algorithms". *Neural Computation*, 8(7), 1341-1390.
    - Extension of NFL to supervised learning.

---

## Financial Mathematics and Portfolio Theory

16. **Markowitz, H.** (1952). "Portfolio Selection". *The Journal of Finance*, 7(1), 77-91.
    - Introduced mean-variance optimization, foundation of modern portfolio theory.

17. **Sharpe, W. F.** (1964). "Capital Asset Prices: A Theory of Market Equilibrium under Conditions of Risk". *The Journal of Finance*, 19(3), 425-442.
    - CAPM model relating risk and return.

18. **Kelly, J. L.** (1956). "A New Interpretation of Information Rate". *Bell System Technical Journal*, 35(4), 917-926.
    - Optimal bet sizing using information theory.

19. **Black, F., & Scholes, M.** (1973). "The Pricing of Options and Corporate Liabilities". *Journal of Political Economy*, 81(3), 637-654.
    - Options pricing via no-arbitrage arguments.

20. **Fama, E. F.** (1970). "Efficient Capital Markets: A Review of Theory and Empirical Work". *The Journal of Finance*, 25(2), 383-417.
    - Efficient Market Hypothesis formulation.

---

## Risk Management and Execution

21. **Rockafellar, R. T., & Uryasev, S.** (2000). "Optimization of Conditional Value-at-Risk". *Journal of Risk*, 2, 21-42.
    - CVaR as coherent risk measure with linear programming formulation.

22. **Almgren, R., & Chriss, N.** (2001). "Optimal Execution of Portfolio Transactions". *Journal of Risk*, 3, 5-40.
    - Optimal trade execution balancing market impact and timing risk.

23. **Jorion, P.** (2006). *Value at Risk: The New Benchmark for Managing Financial Risk* (3rd ed.). McGraw-Hill.
    - Comprehensive VaR methodology and applications.

24. **Thorp, E. O.** (2006). "The Kelly Criterion in Blackjack, Sports Betting, and the Stock Market". In *Handbook of Asset and Liability Management* (pp. 385-428). North-Holland.
    - Practical applications of Kelly criterion.

    - Comprehensive deep learning reference.

26. **Bishop, C. M.** (2006). *Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning*. Springer.
    - Bayesian approaches, graphical models, kernel methods.

## Adversarial Robustness

## Neuroscience and Cognitive Science

30. **Rao, R. P., & Ballard, D. H.** (1999). "Predictive Coding in the Visual Cortex: A Functional Interpretation of Some Extra-classical Receptive-field Effects". *Nature Neuroscience*, 2(1), 79-87.
    - Predictive coding model of visual perception.

31. **Friston, K.** (2010). "The Free-Energy Principle: A Unified Brain Theory?". *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*, 11(2), 127-138.
    - Free energy minimization as organizing principle of brain function.

32. **Clark, A.** (2013). "Whatever Next? Predictive Brains, Situated Agents, and the Future of Cognitive Science". *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*, 36(3), 181-204.
    - Predictive processing framework for cognition.

33. **Hohwy, J.** (2013). *The Predictive Mind*. Oxford University Press.
    - Philosophical implications of predictive processing.

34. **Seth, A. K., & Friston, K. J.** (2016). "Active Interoceptive Inference and the Emotional Brain". *Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B*, 371(1708), 20160007.
    - Active inference applied to emotions and consciousness.

    - Multiple testing problems in factor discovery.
